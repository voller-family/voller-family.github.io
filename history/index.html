Immigration to United States

Emil Roth emigrated to the United States and settled in Sharon, Wisconsin. After his wife, Sophia, died in childbirth, he returned to Germany and returned with Lena Roth. They married in Michigan on March 21, 1903.

Lena's brother, Jacob Voller, came to the United States on August 14th, 1903 at the age of 23. He entered via Ellis Island (New York City) on the S.S. La Savoie with $35 in cash. His documentation lists him as a farmer and unmarried. He joined his brother-in-law, Emil, in Wisconsin and worked on Emil's farm for a year and a half. Next, he went to Chicago to work for a schoolmate, Harry Long, for three months and returned to Wisconsin.
